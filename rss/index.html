Podcast for ECFS312X on edX

This is a prototype podcast for of the videos for Positive Behavior Support for Young Children on edX. This podcast lets you:

  • Watch from virtually any device, including your phone
  • Automatically get new videos as they become available
  • Download overnight on low-bandwidth connections
  • Use your favorite RSS client

edX courses interleave rich assessments, discussions, and other interactive materials with video content. Some of the videos may be out-of-context without those. If you would like the full course experience, please visit the course on edx.org.

This is an engineering prototype.

We make no guarantees on whether this will work correctly, or continue to work correctly. You are the first testers. Whether we decide to launch it in other courses will be based, in part, on your experience and feedback. If you have any feedback about this feed, please let us know at pmitros+rss@edx.org.

Getting started

Used podcasts before? Simply subscribe to: 

http://add-link-when-and-if-course-staff-approves/feed.rss

Need a bit more help? We have instructions for a few different devices below (we're not endorsing the particular apps; they just happened to be the first ones that worked for us):

Subscribing on iPhone and iPad

To subscribe to this podcast on iPad:
  • Install RSS Radio on your device (there's a version for older devices as well).
  • Skip the startup screens, and at the main screen, click on the plus on the top right to add a feed. Hit "more" on the top left, and select "Enter the URL Manually." Cut-and-paste this link: 
    http://add-link-when-and-if-course-staff-approves/feed.rss
    Make sure to hit "subscribe" on the last page.
  • Click on the feed. Select "settings" on the top right (not the app settings, but the feed settings), and change "Download" to "all."
  • Congratulations! You're done! Download and watch a few videos, and enjoy!

Subscribing on Android

  • Install DoggCatcher.
  • On the startup screen, change downloads to 5.
  • Hit "Subscribe", and from the list, select "Feed RSS URL"
  • Scroll down, and enter the URL: 
    http://add-link-when-and-if-course-staff-approves/feed.rss
  • On the main screen, delete all existing feeds. DoggCatcher comes with a bunch, and they'll just waste space and make clutter.
  • Go into the main feed. Hit the download button to download a few videos. Wait for a few videos to download, and hit play.
